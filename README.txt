KENON | ECHON — How to Play

KENON | ECHON is a browser-based ambient drone instrument. There are no wrong notes — everything is tuned to itself. The two engines, Kenon and Echon, can run together or separately, shaped by a shared chopper and master controls in the middle and bottom strips.


VOICE BANKS

The five buttons at the top pick the overall tuning and character of both engines — from warm and melancholy (I) to bright and open (III) to sparse sine tones (V). Switching while playing will crossfade into the new voice. Keyboard: 1–5.


KENON (left panel)

Three large orbs each toggle a sound layer: a thick detuned root tone, a shimmery harmonic layer, and a deep sub-bass. The small orb at the top toggles all three at once. Keyboard: W (master), A / S / D (left, center, right orb).

The three vertical sliders control density (loudness), pitch, and brightness.  

Small buttons below the sliders trigger automated slider behaviors.  

At the bottom, two small circles add plate reverb and delay to Kenon's signal.


ECHON (right panel)

Six pads arranged in an arc, each a note from the current voice's scale. Press and hold to sound a drone, release to let it fade. Click the small dot beneath a pad to lock it on indefinitely. The tiny circle in the top corner kills all active drones. Keyboard: hold J, I, O, P, [, ] (left to right across the arc) — release to stop.

Four knobs control volume, reverb, timbre (the tone's harmonic complexity), and envelope (how slowly notes swell in and fade out). Two switches add delay and flange effects.


CHOPPER (center panel)

Rhythmically gates Kenon and Echon independently. Toggle each section on with its circle button. The three levers control chop rate, how often a beat is skipped, and how many rapid sub-pulses fire within each beat. When active, the pattern evolves on its own. The small circle below each section randomizes the levers.


MASTER CONTROLS

Master Vol and Master Reverb apply to everything. The Rec button records your session and saves it automatically with a single-word filename when you stop.


BLACKHOLE OUTPUT

The K -> BH, E -> BH, and M -> BH buttons in the bottom strip send Kenon, Echon, or the full master mix as separate audio streams to an external destination — useful if you're running the synth inside Electron or a custom host and want to route the signal into a DAW or effects environment like Max/MSP without going through your speakers.
